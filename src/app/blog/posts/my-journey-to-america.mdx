## A Big Leap

Moving from China to the United States was the biggest decision of my life. I left behind everything familiar — my family, my friends, the food I grew up with, and a language I could speak without thinking twice.

When I first arrived in San Francisco, everything felt both exciting and overwhelming. The city was beautiful, diverse, and fast-paced. But I also felt like I was starting from zero.

## Starting Over

One of the hardest parts of starting over in a new country is rebuilding your confidence. In China, I had a degree, work experience, and a social circle. In America, I had to prove myself all over again — in a new language, with a new culture, and with new rules I didn't fully understand yet.

But I've always been a fast learner and a problem solver. I reminded myself that I had already accomplished so much, and I could do it again.

## What I've Done Since Arriving

Since moving to San Francisco, I've kept myself busy and focused:

- **Got my GED** and attended courses at City College of San Francisco
- **Learned coding** through self-study and Decode Academy courses
- **Built websites and automation tools** — proving to myself that I can pick up new skills fast
- **Enrolled in Job Corps** for the Culinary Art program
- **Adopted Gabri**, my puppy who brings joy to every day
- Started attending **morning meditation classes** to stay grounded

## Lessons Learned

This journey has taught me a few things:

1. **It's okay to be uncomfortable.** Growth happens outside your comfort zone.
2. **Being bilingual is a superpower.** I can connect with people from two completely different worlds.
3. **Stay focused on what you can control.** I can't change where I started, but I can choose where I'm going.
4. **Be kind to yourself.** Moving to a new country is hard. It's okay to take it one day at a time.

## What's Next

I'm looking for opportunities in international business, sales, administration, or even software engineering. I want to use my bilingual skills, my marketing experience, and my new coding abilities to build a career that bridges cultures and creates value.

San Francisco already feels like home. And I'm just getting started.
